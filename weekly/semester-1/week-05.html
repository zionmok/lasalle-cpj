“Is this method really the best option for you? Or is it just accessible?”
Types of Interview:
  • 1. Structured Interview
  • > Require deep knowledge about the topic (perhaps after speaking with a professional)
  • > Questions are close-ended

  • 2. Unstructured interviews
  • > Questions prepared
  • > For framing a problem
  • > Common mistake: “ill just have a chat with the people”

  • 3. Semi-structured interview
  • > Suitable for experienced interviewers
  • > open-ended
  • 4. Contextual Inquiry
  • > On-site interviews
  • > Suitable for new products/experience
  • > A bit like a survey?

  • 5. Focus Groups
  • > The group is chosen with predefined demographic traits
  • > Aim to shed light on a topic of interest

  • Tips!
  • > Try to avoid “the interviewer effect”: the quality of your script will allow you to avoid bias and not influence your interviewee.
  • > Also to avoid the “social desirability bias”, meaning that interviewees unconsciously try to please the interviewer by “fitting” in their expectations.
  • > Consent forms for interviewees

    We were separated into groups of 5, and tasked to create a proposal for brands to go online.

    We could either, Launch/Rebrand/Market for the brand.

    I proposed to my group that maybe we could go towards a more traditional brand such as tiger balm or TCM. Isabella suggested local beverage brand YEOs, but I felt that it might difficult for our group members to relate as some are not local. Therefore, we went around the table to talk about our ideas. Yun Jeong mentioned Korea’s drinking culture and we immediately agreed on bringing JINRO Soju into the metaverse.

    We expand the idea into the party tricks that Koreans do when they’re drinking with friends. We wanted to bring that experience of excitement and performance online and allow people all over the world to learn and play with it.

    We then delegated the parts diligently among our group members.

    Virtual party room experience where users can come together from anywhere into this party tricks and other drinking culture experience. Whether it be friends or family, you can bond and share these experiences with your loved ones from anywhere in the world.

    Simulate being tipsy and drunk with the visual effects without actually consuming the alcohol.

    Create any type and mix any flavour of Soju, possibilities are endless.

    Voice recognition, connecting cheers and noise from the outside world to really simulate a real party within the virtual world.
