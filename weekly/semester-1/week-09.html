What’s the difference between RPO and dissertation?
  • In dissertation, you will be expanding from your RPO.
  • Research objective RPO got some questions, in dissertation try to answer them in discussion.
  • After discussion, if you realise that there is still missing information needed for your project, you can add them into “Limitation of Research”. This will act as a hint you are aware that it’s needed but you do not have it right now. This allow readers to understand the missing piece and able to take your research to the next level.
  • Many readers only read introduction and conclusion at first, so you need to summarise your paper in conclusion.
  • Your paper will be more impactful if you end off the paper with a prompt. Perhaps a quote from the interview or a question.
  • Appendix can include a transcript of an interview, a nerdy chart from a professor, or a picture.

    • Kwok Yu Qi helped the readers to understand the purpose of her paper with 3 concise points.

    “In point form, the gap in RPO, then uses the methodology to find out, then discuss in the discussion. Logical right?“

    Process of User Research ↓
  • User research provides an essential foundation for a good design strategy.
  • It helps you to create an optima product/service for users and discover innovation that can exist within unique groups
  • Most importantly, you'll have the data to back your strategy and design decisions.
  • Define research goals
  • Key research questions
  • Prep a discussion guide
  • Having opening questions (warm-up questions)

    • User Research focuses on understanding user expectations, behaviors, needs, and motivations through methodical, investigative approaches.

    The insights are then used toinform business & design decisions, or even question – How might we benefit the user?


    In synthesis, you’re trying to find meaning in your data. This is often a messy proces — and can mean reading between the lines and not taking a quote or something observed at face value. The why behind piece of data is always more important than the what.”

    Post-it Etiquette

    But wait, there’s rules to synthesizing research.

    Rule #1:

    It has to be something you observed, or a quote, or a behavior.


    Rule #2:

    No solutioning allowed. Don’t assume what people need before you know the problem they all have.

    Exercise 01 – 5 Questions (5 mins)

    Define goal(s): How often do students impulse buy during grocery runs?

    5 questions:

  • How do you get your groceries?
  • How to prep yourself before going to the supermarket?
  • Do you go to the supermarket alone or with people? Who do you normally go with?
  • Can you draw a route on this map how would you go about your grocery shopping
  • How often do you buy things that are not on your shopping list? Why do you think you trail off the list?
    • Exercise 02 – Conducting User Interviews

    Tips!

  • Find ways to record the interviews (ask for permission)
  • Make participants comfortable
  • Take observational notes: note should not be solutions, focus on observations, quotes, inferences (use drawings)
  • Be mindful of cognitive bias - You may exhibit the same exact behavior as an interviewee, but you cannot assume their reason for doing so is the same as yours.
  • Guide the conversation, don’t control it. Use active listening techniques to refocus their thoughts without leading the conversation
  • Ask open-ended questions. Uncover feelings, beliefs, and personal stories. Never settle for “yes” or “no” answers.
  • Be prepared to improvise. You never know what people will tell you. If your interview turns into a more organic conversation you may want to roll with it — think of it as a checklist of topics you want to cover.

    • Body language: be open and relax, so that your interviewee can feel the same.

    Findings

  • Freezer items are enticing 90% of students go to grocery stores alone
  • No matter if you have a list or not, students tend to impulse buy
  • When they impulse buy, they imagine a scenario where they will consume the food soon
  • Shopping lists are created to help them remember what to get, instead of restricting themselves

    • Goals

  • Do they impulse buy less when they shop with people?
