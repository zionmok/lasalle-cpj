35 days into this research topic, I’ve accepted that I have to be the biggest nerd towards Design Process. So I researched more on UI/UX methodologies as well. To understand UI/UX designers, I have to familiarise myself with their terms and lingo. Through reading these books and additional articles, I realised that UI/UX methodologies are mostly checklists to use for usability testings. Yes, UI/UX designers should have this lists of items at the back of their mind when making design choices throughout the interface, but it’s so much. how often do designers forget and the product ends up being frsutrating to use?

I hope to find that out during my interviews with UI/UX designers

    DILLAH ZAKBAH (CREATIVE DIRECTOR OF BBH)

    Dillah shared that back in the days where organisations started using advertising, it was for the purpose of propoganda usually.

    It then became a way of getting people to focus and recognise you.

    Instead of “you need this in your life!”, brands started to incorperate meanings and lifestyles along with their products.

  • Because there’s so many ads, no one cares anymore
  • Instead, it should be human-centered, engaging not interruptive
  • Ideas that people want to spend time with “sticks in your brain”
  • Ads becomes a mode of entertainment
    • When you’re in advertising, you’re not just competing with your direct competitors in the same field. You are competing with everything.
