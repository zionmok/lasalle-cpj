Feedback

Inspired by how lecturers like to give us a big piece of paper for groups to brainstorm ideas, I rolled out a piece of mahjong for visitors to write down their feedback. I figured that a big drawing area with some messy writing already on there would make adding some feedback less daunting.

Some of the feedback was told to me during the sharing session, so I wrote it down instead of requiring them to write it down themselves. I feel that many people are interested in UI/Ux, and they were able to provide constructive feedback.


Many commented that the branding was okay but the UX wasn’t up to standard. Some of the GUI doesn't work and it was difficult to navigate.

Throughout the week, I was so inspired by real people that interact with the app, I sat down in front of my laptop every chance I get to work on the feedback I’d gotten. So every single day, there was something different about the app.

first I added guides at the start to introduce the GUI system for the app

I then realised that the guide is too draggy, so I combined it based on swipes and taps.

I then changed the search function into a chat style after talking to a alumni

Previously I copied what chatgpt chat and put it into the speech bubble. But it doesn't make sense for the search function to be dumping information like that.

Read.cv

I'm invited to experience Read.cv!

Just before our senior, Matthew left, he shared with me a new platform that is still on an invitation basis – Read.cv. He so kindly invited me to experience the mobile version of it knowing that it is quite similar to my project. He describes the current stage of it as a Twitter for creatives.

It’s like a world from Wreck-It-Ralph where all the villains are gathered in one room to share about the life of the unlikeable character. It was fascinating. There are sharings of new designs, conversations about existing designs, passion for iconic designs, and even job opportunities.

Podcast

Podcasts are my new favourite pastime.

Thanks to Tiktok, I have discovered some amazing design related podcasts with extraordinary hosts that ask piercing questions.

I was surprised to see this topic come up on the podcast that I listen to regularly. On this episode, Brain Lovin, founder of Campsite, came on to talk about his product that allows design teams to get access to the work-in-progress updates, as well as leave comments and feedback to questions posted by peers.

The host, Jane Portmane, asked piercing questions about the nature of his product and that in turn helped me to question the nature of my product as well. When I am able to answer all these questions with confidence, my product will be even more legitimate and serve a real purpose to the users.
