Experienced Designers Interview

I actually conducted user interviews with 3 experienced designers last year, around October 2022. It took me longer than expected to transcribe as I was relying on my not-so-good memory to keep track of the key points that they have mentioned and some insights I gathered during the interview itself. But I realised that as time go by, I started to doubt my own memory and question if I took it in the right context. Additionally, the conversation with senior Jiarong who works at Deloitte was over Zoom, I realised after that I had a difficult time focusing and processing all the information he gave me. So I finally gave in and decided to stop procrastinating over this. Finally finished the transcript and was able to categorise them according to key theories and examples they shared. Because of that, I could draw parallels much easier compared to when it was hovering in my mind.

Research Statement



Through the holidays, I’ve spent some time here and there thinking about my research statement. Previously it was to uncover the attributes and characteristics of design thinking from the young designers' perspective who are in UI/UX design. The focus was that from sem 1 as I was hoping to go into the field of UI/UX after graduating from LASALLE. But I slowly discover that it is not realistic for me to do so as it is a niche group of people, but the research should be helpful for young designers in general. Therefore, I took a step back and decide to focus on young graphic designers instead.


Consultation

In this consultation, Joselyn gave me some comments from the formative feedback. Some changes here and there to make the sentence flow better, and add some explanations to allow readers to have a better understanding of what the paper is talking about.

One thing I regretted was not preparing a new copy of the key research inquiry and research statement for this consultation as I’d already decided to nudge the research in a slightly different direction, and this research inquiry wasn’t accurately reflecting the direction I want to move my project towards.

Originally, the plan was to conduct user interviews with young designers before Christmas 2022. It dragged on until now and the questions have yet to be vetted and confirmed. I’m starting to panic.



Wasn’t until Joselyn asked me about my plan B that I realised that it might not be realistic for me to find willing young designers to sit down and share with me their personal creative process. Joselyn helped me understand that everything should have a backup plan, in case plan A doesn’t work out. One way I could get some sort of materials if the interviews can’t happen, for whatever reason, was to find published interviews online. Ideally, it would be Singapore graphic designers. Another alternative was to look at Asian graphic designers, although it is slightly different from my intended target audience.

Class Presentation

Class presentations tend to feel more comfortable. A small group of just us who are pretty familiar with each other’s project are updating each other on our progress, at the same time seeking some suggestions for improvement. It feels natural. Therefore, I did not have to prepare much but still manage to bring my idea across.

Some of the feedback received was about the ideation sketches. Most agreed that the idea was inspired by Kahoot! would be interesting to adapt to a designer’s version. If I could explain a bit further about the idea, inspired by the multi-screen icebreaker game Kahoot!, I wanted to design something with multiple screens, mimicking a guided sprint workshop. Where the instructions will be shown on the big screen and the participants can interact with a smaller screen (personal smartphone) to ‘answer’.

However, one thing I’m still struggling to figure out is the user flow of the app. As the feedback reminded me that designers are not friendly with a too systematic process, and that idea would be too mediated for such creative individuals.

If designers are not interested in systematic approaches, I should explore on some of the possible processes that are commonly used by young designers, and hope to draw some insights such as similarities and differences.

This 'game' can help young designers to reflect on their creative process consciously. As it is often intuition for designers to ‘just start designing’. Doing this allows them to look at their creative process as one big picture, and analyse the similarities and differences between their own creative process and their peers.

But, so what if they are able to self-reflect?

Predicted Problems

Although it might sound like a fun idea to plan and execute, I don't think it makes sense for this project. The need for a flexible framework is going to be difficult with all the technological constraints. Designing itself consists of many physical items such as sketches, prototypes, and mindmaps. I just can’t see how I can cater for all of those options on the Kahoot! inspired app.
