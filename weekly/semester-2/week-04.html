Key Research Inquiries

Previously, my key research inquiries were in line with the new research premise I had in mind. So I tasked myself with coming up with new research inquiries.

Before

After

In the previous Key Inquiries, it was clear my stand was on a less rigid design thinking process, and how it benefits young designers. But, after talking to the experienced designers, I felt that there’s really a big difference between the literature research I’ve done and the reality in Singapore. The literature helped me to understand the background of design methods, yes, but in Singapore, designers do it intuitively. So it might not make sense for me to take a stand so fast.

Therefore, for my new key inquiries, I wanted to focus more on understanding and uncovering the subconscious decisions made by young designers in Singapore. Through years of conversations with classmates and colleagues, I know that there are many common struggles that we face as young designers in Singapore. I wanted to focus on the challenges regarding the creative process.

Jiaxiang's Sharing

Previous Next


A few weeks ago after a consultation, Joseyn shared with us about the healthcare industry. Apparently, one of our alumni is currently working at TTSH, service design. And as curious students who don't know much about the design industry, we asked Joselyn if it’s possible to have him as a guest speaker to share more about the healthcare-design industry. Lucky us, he agreed so fast and came down to give us both a sharing and a consultation.

During his sharing, I was inspired by how clearly he articulated his design process. He shared the problem, goals, and general theme of the project. He then talked about where the problem arise from (empathy), the rationale behind the chosen solution, and the part that intrigues me the most, they formed a team of designers and nurses (who are closest to the target audience) to design prototypes of the solution (chair) at the studio. Not only designers were involved in the process, I really admired the effort the nurses put into this project in order to see the visitors be more comfortable on the chair when they are visiting their family members.



I noticed that he used many graphs to visualise the process he and his team goes through for a project. During the whole presentation, he really emphasized sharing his process. Not sure if that’s what designers normally share about or if it’s because we’re students and he thinks that his thought process would be helpful to us. Anyway, it further supports my point about the importance of the design process to a creative.

Week 2’s presentation went pretty well with new ideations and sketches but presenting to someone who has never heard of my project before brought some unexpected challenges and feedback.

Jiarong mentioned that it was pretty difficult for him to understand my project from the deck and my sharing due to the lack of clear objectives and goals for the project. The ideas presented were all scattered and doesn’t have a theme to them, thus making it difficult for people to understand the purpose of the ideas.

This is very good advice as I’ve only been sharing my projects with my classmates and Joselyn, who has been on this journey with me from the very beginning. This will definitely help me to organise my thoughts clearly for further sharing/presentation.

Key Takeaways:

    > unclear objectives and goals
    > no visible theme to ideas
    > coherency of the presentation is lacking

The ideas started to seem too vague and surface-level. It doesn’t feel like it’s something memorable and could be used continuously outside of the grad show or exhibition.
