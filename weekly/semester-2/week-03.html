Ideation & Sketches

I was inspired by the fortune-telling rock seen in shopping malls in the early 2000s. It analyses your palm and prints out a paper of your fortune. I was always so scared of it when I was younger, perhaps that’s why it left such a deep impression. Through class sharings, it seems like it’s the same for the rest of my classmates who grew up in Singapore.




Using the help of sketches, I attempt to find out the possible user flow for the app. One of the variations is a more systematic approach. This might cause inaccurate analysis as  young designers might be pressured into choosing the ‘correct’ creative process.   Another variation slightly solves this issue. It covers the purpose of those steps with applications. As we rely on technological aids in our creative process, I thought that involving them might lessen the pressured feeling from users. Instead of research, it would be Google. Instead of finding inspiration, it would be Behance. And instead of ideation, it would be sketching. Something like that. So far I’m leaning towards the book idea as there is   a series of deliverable that comes together as a strategy.  


The ideas started to seem too vague and surface-level. It doesn’t feel like it’s something memorable and could be used continuously outside of the grad show or exhibition. The idea was to design something that can remind young designers that they don’t have to do it all themselves. Research has shown that having   a strong support system has many positive benefits,   such as higher levels of well-being, better coping skills, and a longer and healthier life. Studies have also shown that social support can reduce depression and anxiety. A strong support system can often help reduce stress.

The real question is what’s the purpose of the deliverables? Let’s use the magic of pen and paper to find out >>>

Interview Questions (Young Designers)

Phase One

    Google form for requirement check
    Questions
  • Name
  • Current Company
  • Current Position
  • Current job scope
  • Are you trained in design? (design school/course)
  • Years of experience?
  • Do you practice systematic design processes? (explain)
  • Can I please have a short interview with you about your design process? Y/N


    • Phase Two

      Obejctives
  • find out their current design processes if they have (systematic or adhoc)
  • struggles they face? Is it due to the lack of guidance?
  • does your company have their own framework?
  • any difference between your own framework and your company’s
  • have to include my own reflection on the same questions if I want to base my project on myself instead of helping the rest of the world.
  • what do you think is a brief that every graphic designer will know how to tackle? Eg. branding, logo design, name card design, etc?

    • Version 1

  • Does your company practice a systematic design process?
  • Do you usually approach a brief systematically? (Following any kind of steps or process)
  • Which stage of your design process do you find the most challenging? Why?
  • I noticed that your company’s process and your process are similar/differ slightly, why?
  • How often do you see other designers’ processes? As a young designer, are you interested in knowing the design processes others go through?
  • Do you think as a young designer, you can benefit from exploring other designers’ processes? (like adopting a game item build from top players)

    • Version 2

  • Can you share with me the design processes your creative team goes through?
  • What are the steps/methods you use as a designer?
  • Which stage of your design process do you find the most challenging? Why?
  • I noticed that your company’s process and your process are similar/differ slightly, why?
  • How often do you see other designers’ processes?
  • Imagine something that will allow you to know more or work more efficiently, what will that be? How so? Did you experience a similar situation in the past?
  • Do you think it's important for everyone on the team to have the same creative process?
