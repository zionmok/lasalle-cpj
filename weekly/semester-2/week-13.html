The previous week we had an open studio where the public and students are welcome to view our projects, and I met some nice people who helped to test the app that I’ve made on Figma. The most prominent part of the testing revealed the limits of loading videos and media on Figma prototype. In between explaining and user testing, our classmates gathered at a nearby table discussing about our projects, and Gracie suggested that I should try another prototyping tool, Protopie.

At first, it was challenging to navigate through the interaction triggers on Protopie as it is operating on a different logic compared to Figma. I could only compare it to coding.

Youtube video to guide me through the code-lkilke prototyping tool.

Another common feedback I’ve gotten from user testing is that it is difficult to navigate through the app as previously it was purely reliant on GUI (Graphical User Interface), and there was an overload of them – every direction means a different thing.

Now, the app will be relying on GUI for switching between projects (similar to current social media), and everything else would be text-based interactions.

I’ve also decided to add progress bars to indicate ↓

  • 1. Other projects – auto scroll to show other projects
  • 2. Number of processes – the number of bars can also be
  • understood as how complicated the process of the project is
