    Art Direction

    Previous Next

    From the previous cohort presentation, I realised that having a good art direction can really make the whole project look composed. But it’s been very difficult for me to choose the art direction as my mind changes every time I see my slides. So I wanted to focus on trying and settling on an art direction.

    As the project is about different types of graphic designers with different creative processes, I wanted to play with multiple colours, representing the many creative processes that exist in Singapore but still manage to work together seamlessly like a rainbow.

    As round table, open studio and presentations are all nearing, I wanted to make something interactive that I can show to let my audience get a rough idea about my project in a fun way. Therefore I wanted to do up a tiktok filter that will show you the creative process types as an entertaining way to encourage designers to consciously think about their creative process. So I started researching for designs that popular tiktok filter users enjoy. Found out that a tarot card filter is actually pretty similar to what I want to do. So I decided to take a reference for that.

    Combination Typefaces

    Exploring the combination of multiple typefaces to show the different creative processes that exist together harmoniously.

    It was difficult to combine typefaces of different styles and weights, while still ensuring that the overall word looks visually balanced and appealing. So I tried multiple combinations while keeping in mind to make sure the letters should look rather different from each other, obvious enough to tell that they are from different typefaces, but also gel well together that one might not immediately be able to tell there’s something unusual with it.

    • letter 'E' is visibly thinner than the other letters, standing out.

    • Tried to outline it to make it thicker, but it doesn’t seem right. Letter ‘O’ is also starting to look odd.

    • Starting from scratch again. Matching letters one by one manually instead of picking letters that look distinct from their font family.

      Although most of the letters look similar to each other, there are certain letters that look distinctly different, successfully giving a mix-and-match feel to the word.

    Adding Colours

    Besides having combination typefaces, I wanted to include a mixture of colours to further emphasize on the different types of creative processes. Starting with primary colours, I branched out to secondary colours with the shade. And on top of that, I added a few more colours that I liked and thought would be a pop of colour for the selection. From that, there are a total of 8 colours currently.

    After applying it to the text, it seems lively but might be too much to look at, causing confusion and repulsion. So I reduced it to 4 colours that I liked. I tried applying the colours to the letters in sequence at first, looping it every time it was all used up. It seems okay until I had to create a variation of the text.

    Recolour


    However, the colours just don’t seem to match well with one another.. So I picked out some letters that could be a variation of the logo for smaller prints. The letters are ‘D’ ‘I’ ‘P’ ‘A’. These letters will be pronounced as ‘dee-pa’, which is similar to the distinctive syllabus of ‘design’ and ‘persona’.

    Then I applied the 4 different colours to each letter.

    Size Variations


    I started to question if this could be a logo for my project. So I decided to experiment with the sizing and see how it would look in different sizes. It might not be the final name of my project but I just wanted to try playing with the idea of having a combination of typeface and colours as the main logo/icon.

    In 100px(H) and 50px(H), it was getting difficult to see the letters, making it difficult to read. Therefore I experimented with picking out certain letters and piecing them together.

    Initially, I picked out ‘DiPe’ but it sounded nothing like the title. After that, it was changed to ‘DiPa’ as the pronunciation still resembles the original title – ‘Design Persona’.

    Afterwards, I adjusted the leading manually to make it look cohesive. Then, I took a step back and look at ‘DiPa’ as a shape, not just letters. Then I realised that the letter ‘P’ doesn’t really gel well with the letters around it. I tried to make part of ‘A’ loop through ‘P’, hoping it will look like a family. But now the ‘i’ looks really squished between the letters ‘D’ and ‘P’. I attempted to give it more breathing space by increasing the kerning. However, the spacing is now unbalanced. Finally, I sheared the letter ‘P’ to look like the italics of ‘i’. It works!

    Creative Strategy

    The Subconscious Process Of Creativity: Young Designers Edition

    Introduction

      > Design thinking wasn’t created for designers
      > My research has shown that designers tend to design intuitively, seldom aware of their creative process as they go about a brief.

    Issue/Problem

      > The lack of confidence affects the involvement of young designers, afraid of pitching their ideas

    The Challenge

      > The design needs to raise awareness about the different types of the creative process in just young graphic designers.

    Target Audience

      > Young graphic designers with less than 2 years of working experience in a team environment. Creative individuals who are suffering from a lack of self-confidence, which in turn affects their involvement in the creative team’s process.

    Insights

      > Young designers tend to have low confidence in their idea and work
      > The design industry in Singapore is extremely fast-paced, and young designers have to streamline their creative process when they enter the workforce.

    Creative Approach

      > Narrate
      > Evoke

    Big Idea

      > Help to build self-confidence in young designers through practice talking about their ideas
      > Creating a community that acts as a support system for young designers to fall back on during rough times
      > Young designers are already busy playing catch-up with the big world. the project aims to act as a one-stop solution for young designers without needing more time and effort on the user’s part.

    Objective/ Significance

      > Bringing the subconscious creative process to the surface, exposing its strength and weaknesses, in order to allow young designers to grow.
      > Something that is here to stay

    Art Direction/ Tone of Voice

      > Personal
      > Comfortably Colourful

    Deliverables/Media Platforms

      > Tiktok Filter (raise awareness like MBTI test)
      > App (evoke a sense of community, support system)
      > Collated resource (for ideation/inspiration)

    Call-To-Action

      > Young designers would practice confidence building in the app
      > Translating it into their workplace
