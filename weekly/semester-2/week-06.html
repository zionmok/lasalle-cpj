Discussion Points

While staring at the empty section of the discussion, I realise that I’m struggling to keep my mind on a focused research premise. So I went back to the intro and highlight the main point I want to find out and write about. But that still didn’t help with my mind floating into lalaland. After a while, I realised that my brain is still focused on the previous task I was doing, be it studio or a simper task like eating dinner. In order to get my brain involved and excited about the current task, I had to explain it to it like how I’m doing a recap for classmates during sharing sessions. It might be weird but reading the intro out loud every time I’m switching back from another task helps me to gather my thoughts better.

In order to organise my thoughts, I wrote out some key findings from the user interviews conducted before and categorised them into common themes.

As the discussion points were not enough to help me formulate connections between the users brought up and the key findings. Therefore, I had to first bring over some of the key points they brought up, then group them into common categories, very much like Affinity Diagramming during a UX activity to discover patterns.


Even after listing down all the content to include, I still wasn’t sure how to structure it. So I went back to read the examples Joselyn has given from previous years. An ex-student, YuQi’s essay really stood out to me as I was not someone who was familiar with the K-pop scene, furthermore concerts. Although I wasn’t familiar with her topic at all, I was compelled to continue reading her essay to find out the experiences people have of concerts during the pandemic. Thus, I tried to refer to her writing style for the discussion chapter.

Well, it wasn’t working at all. Although it was so effective with her topic, it just didn’t make sense for me to state clear statistics of the research outcome first, and then explain it in-depth with supporting materials.

reference from Kwok Yu Qi

As the key findings for each interviewee is so different, it’s impossible for me to state statistics up front. All of the interviewees were talking about different experiences that they have gathered through their entire design journey, everyone’s is different. But the conclusion ends around the same area. So I decided to ditch the format and used what I learned in secondary school, the PEEL technique.

Conclusion

Right after narrowing down the discussion points, I have a clearer direction that my research will go. Before I lose this state of mind, I wrote down some main points about how I want to conclude.
