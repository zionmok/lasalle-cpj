studio.

"What is Community to you?"

After speaking with Joselyn, we agreed that the TikTok filter is a secondary part of the project. Perhaps I should focus on the main part of the project with is a mobile application for designers.


The first thing I needed to know was,
what is a 'support system' for designers?

Initially, I turned to myself for the answer. Simply because I’m the most accessible designer.

Additionally, I asked some young designers about what a 'support system' mean to them.

Everyone on them has a unique viewpoint on what a ‘support system’ is to designers. But if I could summarise each message,

  • > Feedback (wholesome Reddit)
  • > Inspiration
  • > 24/7 call centre for software-related questions & client problems
  • > Speaking to your bosses about workload
  • > Mental and physical well-being after being neglected due to long hours of design work
  • > Room for creative opportunities such as outsourcing

Special mention

A lot of times, people tell designers how to do their job because the works are so visual which can be frustrating, like, no one tells an accountant how to do their finances

  We need people who understand us.  

Userflow

With these new pieces of information, I created a user flow that I would like the app to provide for young designers.


Feedback

After a sharing session with the class, it was obvious that the app I wanted to make is trying to cover as much support as it can for young designers. But I have to acknowledge that I can’t provide a solution for every problem they encounter.

Joselyn then shared with me that a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) is usually introduced at the start of the problem, targeting a specific problem. Perhaps I should align the features for a more coherent goal.
